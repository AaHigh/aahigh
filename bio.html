ABOUT

Veteran arcade and console developer with credits spanning Nintendo 64, PlayStation 2, Dreamcast, and Nevada-regulated skill gaming. Designed the first SHA-based arcade score verification system for cash-prize competitions at Atari Games in 1999 — the same cryptographic philosophy that drives CELLTOWER's proof-of-human-work architecture today.

Holds a patent for keypad-based player data storage across linked arcade cabinets. Has direct experience navigating Nevada Gaming Control Board process and California skill-gaming law. Independent developer and creative director operating from the Tower District, Fresno CA.

The throughline: verifiable fairness. The math as the trusted party, not the operator.
